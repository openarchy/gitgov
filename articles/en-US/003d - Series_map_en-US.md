## **AFTERWORD: Additional portals to Series 2**

| # | Year | Author | Work / Text | Problem it opens | Position in the series |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| P1 | 2012 + 2024 | Nate Silver | *The Signal and the Noise* (2012) + *On the Edge* (2024) | How to record, alongside each decision, the conditions that would justify reverting it. Without that, the version history exists but the trigger for using it does not. | Portal 1 |
| P2 | 2024 | Ethan Mollick | *Co-Intelligence* (2024) | AI as co-intelligence: raises the floor of participation, but also accelerates fraudulent participation. Do the 21 steps work with artificial agents? | Portal 2 |
| P3 | 2024 + 2026 | Dario Amodei | *Machines of Loving Grace* (Oct. 2024) + *The Adolescence of Technology* (Jan. 2026) | The time window for embedding safeguards before AI concentration makes any structural correction impossible. It is narrow. Not in decades. | Portal 3 |

> **Silver:** *The Signal and the Noise* showed how to separate signal from noise in predictions. *On the Edge* (2024) extended this to environments of extreme uncertainty: traders, professional gamblers, risk analysts. His contribution to governance as versioning: honest decisions include the criteria that would lead to their own undoing. A law that does not define when it should be revised will never be revised.

> **Amodei:** *Machines of Loving Grace* (October 2024) describes the scenario if everything goes right: AI that compresses fifty years of scientific progress into a decade. *The Adolescence of Technology* (January 2026) is the other side of the same coin: the same AI, without adequate safeguards, enables totalitarian control via personalized propaganda and unprecedented concentration of power. The central metaphor: humanity as a teenager who gained adult power before having the maturity to use it. Amodei does not spare his own company from the list of actors that need to be monitored. These two texts together are the most urgent argument for not waiting on Series 2.
